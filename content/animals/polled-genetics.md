## Three calves, three stories

**"Die Hard" (E148D)** is a miracle baby. Four TransOva Angus receipt cows were picked up less than a week before calving and packed into the rear 8-ft section of a gooseneck for a 24-hour, 1,400-mile haul from Iowa to New York. When the gate opened, a half-trampled Wagyu calf with swollen legs and a crushed tail rolled out. He wouldn't nurse; the vet doctored him and the mother was run into the chute to hand-milk colostrum. Bald as could be — no horn buds — so we knew he was polled. Within minutes he stood and walked (more like limped); within a week or two the crushed tail fell off, the swelling went down, and Die Hard was running the pasture like any spring calf.

**"Bruce Willis" (E148B)** had it easier — born on a beautiful spring day in a green pasture a week or so after the Iowa trip. We felt his head, knew he was polled, and since we already had a "Die Hard," his bald brother became "Bruce Willis."

**"G.I. Jane" (E148G)** was a fighter from the start. A flushmate to the other two, her receipt mama took off on her; she was lost in a swampy area for a couple days and found weak, skinny and dehydrated. Once we knew she was polled we caught the wild receipt mama, hand-milked colostrum, and tube-fed her a couple days until she recovered 100%. What better name to go with Die Hard and Bruce Willis than G.I. Jane.
